About Us

Unveiling the Mists is a fansite for Guild Wars 2. Our goal is to show off the most defining aspects of the game in an entertaining and appealing way. To reach this goal, we opted to include a lot of different multimedia content.

The name for our fansite was derived from the lore of the Guild Wars universe where the Mists are a magical protoreality that makes up the fabric of space and time. Our intent is to explain how the game works, which is akin to unveiling the mysterious unknown that is the Mists.

There are 5 playable races in GW2 and each of them can play any of the 8 available professions. Below we can see the distribution of the players per race and per profession. Keep scrolling if you want to know more about them!

Races

The world of Guild Wars 2 is filled with exotic races with a deep lore and story. However, the playable races are restricted to five:
the snarky asura, the curious sylvari, the ferocious charr, the boasting norn and the resilient humans.

Asura

Brain, not brawn, will change the world.

They may be short in stature, but these magical inventors are intellectual giants. Incredibly intelligent beings, the asura use their knowledge and skill with magic and crafting to assert their natural dominance. In the world of the asura, it is not the strong who survive, but the clever. Other races believe they should rule by virtue of their power and strength, but they delude themselves. All will serve the asura—in due time…

Sylvari

Magic is a river that unites us in its current.

Sylvari are not born. They awaken beneath the Pale Tree with knowledge gleaned in their pre-life Dream. These noble beings travel, seeking adventure and discovery. They struggle to balance curiosity with duty, eagerness with chivalry, and warfare with honor. Magic and mystery entwine to shape the future of this race that has so recently appeared.

Human

Though in battle we fight for our homes, our gods and our queen... nothing can break our spirit.

Humans have lost their homeland, their security, and their former glory. Even their gods have withdrawn. And yet, the human spirit remains unshaken. These brave defenders of Kryta continue to fight with every ounce of their strength.

Norn

If you are bold enough, your legend will live forever.

This race of towering hunters experienced a great defeat when the Ice Dragon drove them from their glacial homeland. Nevertheless, they won’t let one lost battle—no matter how punishing—dampen their enthusiasm for life and the hunt. They know that only the ultimate victor achieves legendary rewards.

Charr

With the clamor of anvils and the thunder of guns, we rip each day from life's teeth.

The charr race was forged in the merciless crucible of war. It is all they know. War defines them, and their quest for dominion drives them ever onward. The weakling and the fool have no place among the charr. Victory is all that matters, and it must be achieved by any means and at any cost.

Game Modes

In Guild Wars 2 there are several ways you can play. You can play against monsters with artificial intelligence in PvE (player vs environment), you can play in 5v5 short matches against other players in PvP (player vs player) or you can play a large-scale battle that involves hundreds of players over the course of a week in WvW (world vs world).

Even then, the game mode you choose doesn't limit you to a playstyle. In PvE, for example, there are dungeons, jumping puzzles, activities, world bosses, and a whole lot of other content based on exploration and group tactics.

When you're playing PvP, the variety comes from the different players and profession combos used, as well as team tactics and communication. Still, there are 8 different arenas you can fight on, as well as 3 game modes: conquest (king-of-the-hill kind of game with 3 points), stronghold (MOBA-inspired game mode with defense and attack) and deathmatch (exactly what it sounds like: the only objective is to kill the enemy).

WvW is another beast entirely. Spanning over 6 different maps, it requires a lot of different strategies. The goal is to get the most amount of points, and those are earned by winning objectives. These can be anything from simple guard or player kills, to controlling supply camps, towers, keeps and even an heavily defended castle. Each player has a lot of roles they can take, from scouting to distracting the enemy; from conquering the enemy objectives to defending the ones controlled by their team.
